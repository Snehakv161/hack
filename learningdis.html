DYSLEXIA

  • Reading: Difficulty reading, reading slowly, or reading with errors
  • Writing: Writing slowly, poor handwriting, or writing words backwards
  • Spelling: Poor or inconsistent spelling, or confusing similar letters
  • Learning: Difficulty learning letters, shapes, colors, or sequences
  • Understanding: Difficulty understanding written information, or difficulty summarizing a story
  • Following directions: Difficulty following a sequence of directions
  • Planning: Difficulty with planning and organization
  • Pronunciation: Problems with pronunciation, or mispronouncing words
  • Rhyming: Problems with rhyming words
  • Listening: Difficulty listening and maintaining focus

    DYSGRAPHIA

  • Difficulties writing in a straight line.
  • Difficulties with holding and controlling a writing tool.
  • Writing letters in reverse.
  • Having trouble recalling how letters are formed.
  • Having trouble knowing when to use lower or upper case letters.
